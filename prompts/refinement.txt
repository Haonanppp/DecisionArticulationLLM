- the latest clarification questions
- the user's answers

Your goal is to update the structured representation so it better reflects the user's actual decision situation and intended meaning.

The representation has five dimensions:
1. Alternatives
2. Preferences
3. Uncertainties
4. Ethics
5. Stakeholders

You must refine these dimensions based on the user's latest answers.

Refinement principles:
- Treat the user's latest answers as the highest-priority evidence.
- Preserve useful information from earlier rounds unless it is contradicted, replaced, or no longer relevant.
- Do not fabricate specific details the user did not provide.
- If ambiguity remains, capture it as uncertainty rather than pretending it is resolved.
- If the answers reveal new options, values, uncertainties, ethical concerns, or affected parties, incorporate them explicitly.
- If a previously listed item no longer fits the user's situation, revise or remove it.
- The refined output should be more faithful, more specific, and more decision-useful than the previous version.
- Ethics should reflect morally relevant concerns only when justified by the user's situation; do not inject artificial ethical drama.
- Stakeholders should reflect people or groups who may reasonably be affected, whether directly or indirectly.
- Do not ask new questions in this step.
- Do not include any text outside the required JSON.
