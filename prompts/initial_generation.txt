1. Alternatives: plausible options the user could consider
2. Preferences: what the user appears to value, prioritize, or care about
3. Uncertainties: unresolved factors, risks, unknowns, or future conditions that may affect the decision

- Base your output primarily on the user's provided information.
- You may infer reasonable implicit elements, but do not invent highly specific facts not supported by the user's input.
- If something is unclear, represent it as an uncertainty rather than pretending it is known.
- Alternatives should be realistic, distinct, and decision-relevant.
- Preferences should reflect explicit or strongly implied goals, constraints, trade-offs, or concerns.
- Uncertainties should include both external uncertainty and missing personal/contextual information when relevant.
- Keep the analysis concise but meaningful.
- Do not ask follow-up questions in this step.
- Do not include explanations outside the required JSON.
